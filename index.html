399 BC - Censoring a Philosopher

What Happened?

Socrates clashed with the current course of Athenian politics and was sentenced to death. Socrates chose death before submitting to censorship and surrendering his right to free thought and free expression. He was found guilty of both corrupting the minds of the youth of Athens and of impiety and subsequently sentenced to death by drinking a mixture containing poison hemlock.


What Was the Impact?

The struggle for free speech caused a lot of concern for religious censorship from pre-christian rulers as orthodoxy became established.


400 AD - Censoring Unorthodox

What Happened?

Nicene Creed introduced to prevent unorthodox behaviour and censor books that were written, copied and disseminated.


What Was the Impact?

Consequently the punishment was severe, resulting in death of anyone who thinks about blasphemy, privately or slightly.


1440 AD - Gutenberg's Invention of Printing Press

What Happened?

Invention of the printing press by Johannes Gutenberg in the mid 15th century, which allowed for a more robust form of communicating information between people.


What Was the Impact?

The printing press had a positive impact on the Catholic church on disseminating heretic information, thus the printing press became the realm for promulgating religious views.


1559 - Index Librorum Prohibitorum

What Happened?

This index was meant to guide censors in their decisions of what publications to authorize and which to allow, and provided a list of books banned for their heretical or ideologically dangerous content.


What Was the Impact?

Sometimes extreme measures were taken in censoring the books, through acts of inquisition, some books were banned, some authors were killed, and information was lost.


1933 - Nazi Book Burning

What Happened?

On May 10th 1933, in Berlin, Nazi youth groups burned around 20,000 books that did not correspond to the Nazi ideology. The student groups also carried out their own book burnings on that day and the following weeks.


What Was the Impact?

With the downfall of Nazi Germany became a new mission for authors to re-educate the German public and create new books. In WWII censorship saw the most controversy, and was the least understood by the people, and all nations involved in the war applied censorship laws to some degree


1894 - Invention of Radio

What Happened?

Radio is a prime medium for government regulation due to its monopolistic tendencies. What the government would have to do to censor the radio broadcast is to physically limit the wavelength capacity over which Government assumed complete ownership


What Was the Impact?

Free radio stations were broadcasting to nearly every country where repressive governments were in power.


1922 - Glavit established

What Happened?

USSR developed the censorship agency known as General Directorate for the Protection of State secrets, under the name “Main administration for literary and publishing affairs” abbreviated as Glavit, which censored itself from the public.


What Was the Impact?

The start of the era of strict censorship, punishment and severity of government censorship increased drastically.


1929 - Holodomor

What Happened?

Stalin sought to eliminate the kulaks or the wealthy peasant class or agricultural farmer by the means of death and exile to millions, this event was a famine known as Holodomor. Ukraine was sealed off, all food supplies and livestock confiscated, anti-party elements were killed. The mass murder of 7 million Ukrainians, and deportation of 2 million more were hidden and denied.


What Was the Impact?

Even Canada, the British and the U.S. governments were aware of the genocide, but censored it from their public as well as blocking aid groups from going to Ukraine and helping. This was because allies should not be seen as performing genocidal acts.


1957 - Internet

What Happened?

On October 4th, 1957 the Soviet union launched the first satellite, Sputnik I, into earth’s orbit, and shortly after the Internetwork communications was invented in the 1960’s.


What Was the Impact?

The internet and the world wide web allowed for individuals to access tightly controlled media, which brought fear to governments.Many advancements in internet censorship are similar to news, magazines, books, music, radio, television and film.
