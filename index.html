399 BC - Censoring a Philosopher

What Happened?

Socrates clashed with the current course of Athenian politics and was sentenced to death. Socrates chose death before submitting to censorship and surrendering his right to free thought and free expression. He was found guilty of both corrupting the minds of the youth of Athens and of impiety and subsequently sentenced to death by drinking a mixture containing poison hemlock.


What Was the Impact?

The struggle for free speech caused a lot of concern for religious censorship from pre-christian rulers as orthodoxy became established.


400 AD - Censoring Unorthodox

What Happened?

Nicene Creed introduced to prevent unorthodox behaviour and censor books that were written, copied and disseminated.


What Was the Impact?

Consequently the punishment was severe, resulting in death of anyone who thinks about blasphemy, privately or slightly.


1440 AD - Gutenberg's Invention of Printing Press

What Happened?

Invention of the printing press by Johannes Gutenberg in the mid 15th century, which allowed for a more robust form of communicating information between people.


What Was the Impact?

The printing press had a positive impact on the Catholic church on disseminating heretic information, thus the printing press became the realm for promulgating religious views.


1559 - Index Librorum Prohibitorum

What Happened?

This index was meant to guide censors in their decisions of what publications to authorize and which to allow, and provided a list of books banned for their heretical or ideologically dangerous content.


What Was the Impact?

Sometimes extreme measures were taken in censoring the books, through acts of inquisition, some books were banned, some authors were killed, and information was lost.


1775 - Lisbon Earthquake

What Happened?

In 1775, there was a major earthquake in Lisbon, Spain. Fake news pamphlets known as relações de sucessos were distributed by churches. In these pamphlets, non-religious people were blamed for the Lisbon earthquake, and claimed that survivors of the earthquake owed their lives to the Virgin Mary.


What Was the Impact?

As the printing industry grew, so did the instances of fake news. Religious institutions had much of the societal power and used printing presses to spread fake news to undermine their oppopnents.


1800's - American Slavery

What Happened?

America in the 1800’s was an environment of mass slavery for African-Americans. Unfortunately, fake news articles claimed that African-Americans were spontaneously turning white and that slave uprisings were popping up across America.


What Was the Impact?

Massive violence against African-Americans and the enslavement of generations.


1835 - The Moon Hoax

What Happened?

In 1835, reporter Richard Adams Locke for the New York Sun had published an article that is now famously known as The Moon Hoax. This article claimed that there was an entire alien civilization living on the moon. At the time, the New York Sun was regarded as a highly credible news publication agency.


What Was the Impact?

By the fifth day of the Moon Hoax publication on the front page of the New York Sun, the story had become extremely popular. Daily sales had gone up from 4,000 copies a day to 19,000 copies a day.


1894 - Invention of Radio

What Happened?

Radio is a prime medium for government regulation due to its monopolistic tendencies. What the government would have to do to censor the radio broadcast is to physically limit the wavelength capacity over which Government assumed complete ownership


What Was the Impact?

Free radio stations were broadcasting to nearly every country where repressive governments were in power.


1899 - The Great Mammoth Hoax

What Happened?

In 1899, the narrator H.Tukeman described hunting a mammoth somewhere in Alaska. Although at this point, mammoths had long been extinct. In the article, H.Tukeman suggested that this may have been one of the last mammoths to exist.


What Was the Impact?

Large numbers of people were fooled by this article and resulted in many of them expressing outrage to authorities


1900's Propaganda - Norman Rockwell

What Happened?

Norman Rockwell is known for his painting called “The Problem We All Live With”. The subject of this painting was the integration of black children in American schools. It is a recreation of an incident regarding a small black girl by the name of Ruby Bridges. Ruby was walking to her school when a crowd of 150 white men and women had gathered to throw tomatoes and yell insulting comments at the young girl.


What Was the Impact?

His pieces were famous for making people feel uncomfortable. This would result in dramatic social change for the United States.


1922 - Glavit established

What Happened?

USSR developed the censorship agency known as General Directorate for the Protection of State secrets, under the name “Main administration for literary and publishing affairs” abbreviated as Glavit, which censored itself from the public.


What Was the Impact?

The start of the era of strict censorship, punishment and severity of government censorship increased drastically.


1929 - Holodomor

What Happened?

Stalin sought to eliminate the kulaks or the wealthy peasant class or agricultural farmer by the means of death and exile to millions, this event was a famine known as Holodomor. Ukraine was sealed off, all food supplies and livestock confiscated, anti-party elements were killed. The mass murder of 7 million Ukrainians, and deportation of 2 million more were hidden and denied.


What Was the Impact?

Even Canada, the British and the U.S. governments were aware of the genocide, but censored it from their public as well as blocking aid groups from going to Ukraine and helping. This was because allies should not be seen as performing genocidal acts.


1933 - Nazi Book Burning

What Happened?

On May 10th 1933, in Berlin, Nazi youth groups burned around 20,000 books that did not correspond to the Nazi ideology. The student groups also carried out their own book burnings on that day and the following weeks.


What Was the Impact?

With the downfall of Nazi Germany became a new mission for authors to re-educate the German public and create new books. In WWII censorship saw the most controversy, and was the least understood by the people, and all nations involved in the war applied censorship laws to some degree


1950's Propaganda - Cold War

What Happened?

By the 1950’s America and Russia were entering an ideological war called “The Cold War”. During this time, both countries were producing propaganda to their respective citizens regarding democracy vs. socialism.


What Was the Impact?

An example of American propaganda against communism are the “Children’s Crusade against Communism” bubblegum cards. These cards were often bought by children and used bright colors with easily understandable language. Propaganda was often target towards children because of their easily deceived minds; and was used often in schools.


1951 Propaganda - Duck and Cover Video

What Happened?

The American federal government’s National Defense team had released the famous “Duck and Cover” (1951) video to educate children on the survival tactics in the event of a nuclear attack. In the video, there is a friendly cartoon turtle named Burt who avoids danger by ducking and covering.


What Was the Impact?

The minds of young children were decieved into thinking that hiding underneath a table would keep them safe in the event of a nuclear strike on American soil.


1957 - Internet

What Happened?

On October 4th, 1957 the Soviet union launched the first satellite, Sputnik I, into earth’s orbit, and shortly after the Internetwork communications was invented in the 1960’s.


What Was the Impact?

The internet and the world wide web allowed for individuals to access tightly controlled media, which brought fear to governments.Many advancements in internet censorship are similar to news, magazines, books, music, radio, television and film.


1914 - German Linguistic Propaganda in WW1

What Happened?

Germans begin replace “Gutten Tag” with “Gott Strafe England” which means “God Punish England”


What Was the Impact?

Greeting one another with "God Punish England" was a unifier for the Germans against their wartime foe. Imagine greeting eachother with "fuck (insert country)" instead of "Hello". This was a great success for the German propgandists. Over the rest of the war, the German government employed very effective internal propaganda (and less effective external propaganda).


1916 - Atrocity Propaganda

What Happened?

Great Britain and its allies use "atrocity propaganda". This means they were depicting the Germans committing terrible acts (whether they were accurate, exaggerated, or fake)


What Was the Impact?

Atrocity propaganda eventually led the U.S into the war. This propaganda was effective enough to convince those in the U.S (which had a large population of Germans) to go to war against Germany after having taken a neutral stance.


1917 - British Monarchy Name Change

What Happened?

King George V, by royal proclomation, changed the Royal Family's name from "Saxe-Coburg and Gotha" to "Windsor"


What Was the Impact?

The impact is the name change. The influence of WW1 caused the public to be anti-German to the point that they no longer wanted a German name associated with their King.


1917 - The First Red Scare

What Happened?

Russian Revolution causes fears of communist revolutions in other countries.


What Was the Impact?

The U.S responded by stoking fears of radical leftist movements by broadcasting messages over the radio. This was the initial onslaught against communism in the U.S.


1936 - Hollywood Anti-Nazi League Founded

What Happened?

The Hollywood Anti-Nazi League was founded in 1936 by Otto Katz and others in the American film industry


What Was the Impact?

Anti-Nazi movies began to proliferate - eventually this leads to support for action against Nazi Germany (in a neutral, war-weary U.S.). The public begins to see depictions of Nazis and Nazism and their disapproval grows.


1939 - Great Britain's Ministry of Information is Revived

What Happened?

Britain revives the Ministry of Information for WW2.


What Was the Impact?

The M.O.I. was Britain's propaganda arm. They started and maintained propaganda campaigns for use against British citizens and others in non-occupied neutral countries. Propaganda at home kept morale up whereas propaganda in neutral countries attempted to sway their neutrality.


1939 - World's Fair in New York City

What Happened?

New York City hosts the World's Fair in 1939. Great Britain was granted an exhibition there.


What Was the Impact?

Great Britain used this as a means to help sway the U.S into another war against the Germans. The Magna Carta and other British cultural icons were put on display. This, a visit from the King and Queen, and other propaganda in the U.S. eventually swayed the war-weary U.S. public into supporting another war against Germany.


1947 - The Second Red Scare

What Happened?

The U.S. enters a period of extreme anti-communism following WW2.


What Was the Impact?

The U.S. employed multiple forms of anti-communist propaganda in a campaign led by Senator McCarthy (hence the name McCarthyism). The propaganda campaigns were so effective that the U.S, to this day, is wary of communist policies. This is most notable in their politics - where "socialist" is deemed an insult. If the Red Scare was less effective, Bernie Sanders may have won the presidential election.


2001 - George Bush Email Hoax

What Happened?

In 2001, the British newspaper The Guardian had published an article regarding a hoax emailing that was circulating soon after the election of President George W.Bush. The email claimed that a study by the Lowenstein Institute of Scranton, Pennsylvania found that President Bush had the lowest IQ of any President in American history with an IQ of 91.President Bill Clinton supposedly had an IQ of 182. This proclaimed study was an absolute hoax and the Lowenstein Institute of Scranton, Pensilvannia does not even exist.


What Was the Impact?

George Bush's credibility as a worthy President was scrutinised. The Guardian published a retraction of the article two days after it was published.


2016 - Fake News influences American Election

What Happened?

A major theme of the recent 2016 American election was the public scrutiny of some news outlets by presidential candidate Donald Trump.During his campaign, he accused the New York Times, CNN, Buzzfeed, and others as being “Fake News”. These networks all have online presences and are competing amongst each other to publish the most breaking news.


What Was the Impact?

Unfortunately, because these networks are often rushed in publishing such articles, more and more often, we see cases of inaccurate reporting. A few days before the 2016 American election, many news outlets published stories favoring the democrats to win the election. The Huffington Post had placed the odds of a Hilary Clinton win at 98.2 percent. The predicted Electoral College votes were 323 for Clinton to 215 for Trump. As we all know, this was not the result of the election.


2017 - Social Media

What Happened?

One fake political news story published by ABCNews received 2,177,000 Facebook shares was titled, “Obama Signs Executive Order banning The Pledge of Allegiance in Schools Nationwide”. The Denver Guardian published an article that received 567,000 Facebook shares titled, “FBI Agent Suspected in Hilary Email Leaks Found Dead in Apparent Murder-Suicide.” The list of examples goes on and on, and with the advent of the numerical counter for sharing and likes on Facebook, this gives an insight into the amounts of people being deceived by this fake news.


What Was the Impact?

With the invention of the internet, the spread of news whether real or fake has gotten so much quicker. People are able to share news through social media, however; groups with ill-intent can spread fake news quickly and to a mass audience. As a result, major social media website’s such as Facebook and Google have announced that they will be cracking down on fake news sources being advertised on their networks.
